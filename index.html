DHAALU ATOLL

Dhaalú Atoll is one of the atolls of the Maldives. It corresponds to the natural atoll of the same name. Dhaalu Atoll stretches 23km (14 mi) from east to west, and 38km (24 mi) north to south. Out of a total of 56 islands in the atoll, six of the islands are inhabited, with a total population of around 10,635. The capital island is Kudahuvadhoo, located at the atoll's southern tip.

Dhaalu Atoll is accessible through regular domestic flights from Velana International Airport to Dhaalu Airport located on Kudahuvadhoo as well as local ferry services.

The atoll is home to several luxury tourist resorts including St Regis Vommuli Maldives, Niyama Private Islands Maldives, Baglioni Resort Maldives, Kandima Maldives, Angsana Velavaru, Vilu Reef, and RIU Palace.

ULHUN DHAALU

A PUBLIC-PRIVATE PARTNERSHIP THAT AIMS TO MAKE DHAALU THE MOST LIVEABLE ATOLL IN THE MALDIVES

The ULHUN DHAALU initiative was founded by the Dhaalu Kudahuvadhoo Local Government and Reseum, with the aim of making Dhaalu Atoll the most liveable atoll in the Maldives. The project leverages international and local collaborative partnerships, to work together to achieve rapid development and raise the quality of life in the islands.

BECOMING A MODEL ATOLL FOR THE REST OF THE MALDIVES

We aim to make DHAALU an inspiration for the rest of the Maldivian atolls.
